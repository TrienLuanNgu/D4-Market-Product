Use case

Cinematic camera motion without professional cinema gear.

DollySync is built for creators who want a polished dolly-zoom shot using an iPhone, a compact rail, and simple physical controls. The operator taps the subject in the companion app, presses a hardware button, and the slider synchronises physical travel with live camera zoom.

The expo pitch focuses on a clear before-and-after message: the effect usually needs coordinated camera movement and lens control, while this artefact turns that coordination into a repeatable one-person workflow.

Final assembly

The completed build uses twin steel rails, a phone carriage, ESP32 firmware, BLE updates, and an iOS app that adjusts video zoom in real time.

Brand system

A technical brand with a clear cinematic payoff.

The identity uses precise rail geometry, a strong action name, and film-production language so visitors understand both the mechanism and the creative result.

DollySync

The mark combines a camera frame with a travelling rail line. It stays simple enough for a poster corner, phone screen, sticker, or QR handout.

Positioning

"Tap. Slide. Zoom." explains the value in one line: subject calibration, physical motion, and synchronised zoom.

Material design

The expo materials use matte charcoal, clean white space, action red, and signal teal to feel like camera hardware rather than a classroom poster.

Justification

The strategy leads with the finished shot, then uses the exposed hardware to prove novelty: software zoom replaced the fragile stylus approach, and stepper travel replaced unstable servo motion.
